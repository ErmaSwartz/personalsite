Description

This project examines the successes and shortcomings of New York City’s 2015 Zero Waste Plan. Utilizing city data, it highlights areas of achievement, identifies opportunities for improvement, and pinpoints critical failures. By analyzing socio-economic factors, consumption patterns, spatial dynamics, income levels, and zoning regulations, the project provides a comprehensive understanding of the “garbage epidemic” and demonstrates how current policies fall short in addressing the scale of the challenge.

Methodology

The methodology focused on three core components:

  • Data Collection: Aggregated waste management reports, city GIS datasets, Census Data
  • Spatial Analysis: Used GIS to map waste disposal locations and their proximity to affected communities.
  • Visualization: Created infographics and maps to communicate findings to policymakers and stakeholders.
